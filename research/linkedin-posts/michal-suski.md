## Post 2
**Date:** 2 Months ago

AI SEO in 2026 isn’t “new SEO.” It’s a new buyer behavior.

Just wrapped a Surfer Academy session with Steve Toth — and the biggest takeaways were refreshingly practical:

AI search is probabilistic → no more “stable rankings,” just directional visibility + share of answers.

Citations win → strong intro + outro, and a table / structured middle that’s easy for LLMs to extract.

Don’t optimize only for discovery → optimize for deal breakers (integrations, budget, constraints, fit) — that’s where decisions happen.

The entire research journey collapses into one window → AI qualifies your brand before anyone clicks.

Omnipresence matters → it’s not just your site: LinkedIn, G2, Reddit (and sometimes indexable social content).

KPIs go back to basics → revenue / leads + “where did you find us?” (self-attribution), because attribution will only get harder.

If I had to summarize it in one sentence:
The best content doesn’t win — the most citable content + consistent brand representation does.

## Post 3
**Date:** 4 Months ago

Stop obsessing over word count. It’s the wrong metric.

We analyzed 57,000+ URLs to see why Google's AI picks one source over another.

The winner wasn't always the longest article. It was the densest.

The findings are clear: 

Pages cited in AI Overviews contain 29% to 62% more facts than non-cited pages.

AIOs don't just want text; they want "information gain." They reward content that connects the dots and covers the full scope of a topic, rather than just skimming the surface.

If you want the citation, you can't just write more. You have to say more.

You have two options:

- Manually cross-reference every competitor to find the gaps in your logic.
- Use Insert Facts in Surfer (smarter and much faster)

It scans the topic, spots the missing context, and adds it to your content in a single click. Not only based on SERP but LLM sources too!
