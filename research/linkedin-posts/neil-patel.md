## Post 1
**Date:** 15-April-2026

The debate over paid and SEO is silly.

One isn't better than the other. It's about optimizing for profitability. If a marketing channel is profitable, leverage it.

If it isn't, try to make it profitable, and if you can't, then consider cutting it.

To top it off, when you look at the data, in most cases, companies that leverage both paid and organic channels see their overall conversion rate increase due to brand growth.

## Post 3
**Date:** 6 Days ago

GEO traffic is the highest converting traffic I've seen over the last 24 years.

But most marketers are about to get a rude awakening.

When we look at how most companies are increasing their GEO traffic, it's by publishing listicles on their own site.

And no shocker, they list their products or services as the best.

But if it is that easy, what do you think will happen?

LLMs are going to recommend bad products and services, users are going to complain, and then their algorithms magically change.

Plus, creating 100s of those listicles and publishing them on your site can really mess up your SEO traffic.

Ideally, you should focus on a long-term strategy that helps both your GEO and SEO results.
